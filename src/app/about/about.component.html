My Programming Journey

After earning a BA in Communication in December 2012, I moved to Seattle, WA where I spent a few years working in sales and advertising. I was first introduced to coding, by chance, when some helpful coworkers showed me a few tricks I could use to automate my work tasks. My interest was sparked. If a few simple statements could have such an effect on my productivity, what else could I do with code?

Eager for more, I began taking introductory programming courses at a local community college each day after work. My interest grew, and in 2015 I decided I wanted to pursue programming professionally. The program instilled CS concepts, emphasizing the application development process by focusing on three main areas: web, mobile, and cloud technologies. I completed my studies in May of 2018, earning a BAS in Application Development.

During my time in school, I worked on various client projects coordinated by the degree program, and completed an internship with a small software company working on cost accounting integrations for Microsoft Dynamics GP. I was kept on as a developer when my internship completed, and in the summer of 2018, I relocated to Taiwan with my husband, and continued with the same company remotely. In Taiwan, I began teaching high school computer science part-time, in addition to my remote work.
